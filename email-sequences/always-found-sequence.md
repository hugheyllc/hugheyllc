## Email 1 — Day 1
**Subject:** One thing to do in your GBP today

Hey [First Name],

Thanks for grabbing the audit checklist.

Here's one thing you can do in the next 20 minutes that will immediately improve your Google Business Profile: add your services.

Most law firms fill in the basics — name, address, phone — and stop there. The Services section is where you tell Google exactly what you practice, with a description for each. If it's empty (or just has a default category), Google has to guess. It usually guesses wrong.

Here's how to do it:

1. Log into your Google Business Profile at business.google.com
2. Click "Edit profile" → "Services"
3. Add each practice area as a service
4. Write a 200-300 character description for each — what you handle, who you serve, where you practice

Keep the descriptions factual. No superlatives, no claims you can't back up. Just what the practice area covers and who the right client is.

This takes 20-30 minutes and it's one of the higher-leverage GBP tasks in my experience working with firms. Google uses service data directly in ranking for practice-area specific queries.

Here's a pattern I see consistently working with law firms:

Meanwhile, a competitor with half their track record is in position one.

The difference is almost never quality of work. It's infrastructure. Specifically:

- Their Google Business Profile is incomplete or mis-categorized
- Their citations are inconsistent (one listing has a suite number, one doesn't, one has the old phone number)
- They have no location pages — just a homepage with their city in the footer
- They haven't asked a client for a review in six months

None of this is hard to fix. It's just tedious, and most firms never work through it systematically.

What makes it costly is that local search has a compounding quality — the firm that owns the top spot tends to stay there. Every week you're not building the infrastructure is a week your competitor is consolidating their position.
