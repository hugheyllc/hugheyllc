A direct review link takes the client straight to the review form. One click, done.

Here's how to get yours:

1. Search for your firm name on Google
2. Find your Business Profile
3. Click "Get more reviews" (in the profile management view) or use this URL format: `https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID`

Your Place ID is in your Google Business Profile dashboard under "Profile information."

Save that link. Put it in your email signature, your intake follow-up email, your case close email. You'll see the difference in response rate immediately.

## Email 2 — Day 3
**Subject:** What review velocity actually does to your rankings

Hey [First Name],

Here's something I see consistently with law firm Google rankings: review velocity matters more than most firms realize — and it's one of the few ranking signals you have direct control over.

Google doesn't just look at how many reviews you have. It looks at how recently you've been getting them, and how consistently they're coming in. A firm that got 80 reviews three years ago and has added two since then is weaker than a firm with 30 reviews that's adding 3-4 per month consistently.

This matters for two reasons:

**Rankings:** Google interprets consistent review velocity as a signal that the business is active, legitimate, and currently serving clients. Stale profiles — even ones with strong historical review counts — tend to drift.

**Client behavior:** What I've seen consistently is that prospective clients read reviews differently from search results and from direct referrals. When someone finds you through Google and you have recent reviews, they're reading them to calibrate expectations. Recency matters to them too — a review from last month reads differently than one from 2022.

The firms that are difficult to displace in local search have usually built a systematic review process into their case close workflow. Not a one-time push. A repeatable system.
