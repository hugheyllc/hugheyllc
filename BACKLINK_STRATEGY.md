## Tier 3: HARO / Journalist Sourcing (Ongoing)

HARO (Help a Reporter Out) and Qwoted connect journalists with expert sources. Joe responding to legal marketing queries gets him cited in news articles — with backlinks.

### Setup
1. Sign up at helpareporter.com (free tier) and qwoted.com
2. Filter for: legal, marketing, small business, entrepreneurship
3. Respond to any query touching: law firm marketing, attorney advertising, legal marketing ROI, intake optimization, legal technology

### Response Formula
Keep responses under 150 words. Lead with the insight, not the bio.

> **[Direct answer to the question in 1-2 sentences]**
>
> [2-3 sentences of supporting explanation with a specific example or data point]
>
> —Joe Hughey, Founder, Hughey LLC (law firm marketing consultant, St. Petersburg FL) | hugheyllc.com

**High-value query categories to prioritize:**
- "Marketing agency red flags" / "How to vet a marketing agency"
- "Law firm marketing trends"
- "Small business marketing mistakes"
- "Marketing ROI measurement"
- "Cost per acquisition strategies"

Target: 5 HARO/Qwoted responses per week. Expected conversion: 1-2 citations/month with consistent effort.

## Notes on What NOT to Do
- No link exchanges / paid links — Google penalizes these
- No generic "write for us" directory submissions
- No spammy comment backlinks
- No PBN (private blog network) services
- No services that promise "100 links in 30 days" — these are always garbage

The strategy above builds real authority that compounds. One Above the Law guest post is worth more than 500 directory submissions.
