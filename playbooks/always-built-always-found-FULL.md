# Chapter 1: Why Your Website and GBP Are Your Foundation

## The Two Assets That Control Your Online Visibility

When someone searches for a lawyer — whether it's "personal injury attorney near me" or "criminal defense lawyer Tampa" — Google returns two types of results:

1. **Google Business Profile** (the local 3-pack or Map Pack)
2. **Your website** (organic search results)

Most law firms treat these as separate. They're not. Your website and GBP are two halves of the same system. GBP drives Map Pack visibility, your website drives organic visibility, and together they capture the overwhelming majority of local legal search traffic.

Here's what matters: **the same ranking factors that make GBP work also make your website work.** The difference is how they're delivered.

## The Three Ranking Factors (For Both)

Google ranks both GBP listings and websites using three core factors:

### 1. Relevance
How well your business (or specific page) matches what someone is searching for.

**For GBP**, relevance comes from:
- Your primary and secondary categories
- Your business description and services section
- Your photos and posts
- The keywords in your GBP fields

**For your website**, relevance comes from:
- Page titles and headers that match search queries
- Page content that directly answers what people search for
- Practice area pages and location pages
- Internal linking structure that shows relationships

**Example:** Someone searches "personal injury lawyer Tampa." Google checks:
- Does your GBP say you handle personal injuries in Tampa? (Yes = more relevant)
- Do you have a website page about personal injury law that mentions Tampa? (Yes = more relevant)
- Do your practice area pages and location pages use those exact terms? (Yes = even more relevant)

Irrelevance is the biggest reason firms don't rank. A firm with a generic website and a vague GBP business description will never rank for specific practice areas, no matter how many backlinks they have.

### 2. Distance
How physically close you are to the searcher or the location they specified.

You cannot move your office, but you *can* influence distance signals through:
- Having a verified physical address in your GBP
- Creating location-specific pages for every area you serve
- Consistent name, address, and phone (NAP) citations across the web
- Mentioning specific cities and regions on your website

A firm three miles from a searcher who has strong relevance signals will outrank a firm five blocks away with weak signals. Distance matters less than you think.

### 3. Prominence
How well-known, trusted, and active your business is.

Google measures prominence through:
- **Your GBP activity:** Posts, photos, Q&As, updates
- **Review signals:** Volume, rating, recency, velocity, response rate
- **Website authority:** Domain age, backlinks, content depth
- **Engagement signals:** Click-through rates, time on site, calls from GBP
- **Citations:** How many reputable directories list your firm with consistent information

Prominence is where most of your work happens. It's also where you can overtake competitors who are geographically closer or had more historical ranking.

## Why Website + GBP Together Is Unbeatable

Here's the compound effect: a firm that has both a strong website *and* a strong GBP captures the search results in a way that's hard to compete against.

**Scenario:** Someone searches "personal injury lawyer Tampa"

Your firm shows up:
1. In the Map Pack (from GBP optimization)
2. In organic results position 1-3 (from website optimization)
3. On the Knowledge Panel (Google builds this from both sources)

Your competitor with only a good website might rank organically but not in the Map Pack. Your other competitor with only a good GBP might appear in the Map Pack but have no website traffic. You get both.

Most law firm competitors are not doing both simultaneously. They're choosing one or the other, or doing both poorly. This is your opportunity.

## The Role of Website Platform in All This

Your choice of website platform — whether you use WordPress, Astro, Wix, Squarespace, or something else — directly impacts your ability to implement the ranking factors above.

Some platforms make it easy to:
- Build fast, mobile-optimized sites (impacts ranking)
- Create multiple relevant pages (impacts relevance)
- Add proper schema markup (impacts prominence)
- Update content quickly (impacts recency)

Other platforms make these things hard, slow, or expensive.

Chapter 3 covers this decision in detail. For now, understand: your platform choice matters because it either enables or blocks your ability to execute the strategy in this playbook.

# Chapter 2: Google Business Profile — Complete Setup

Your Google Business Profile is the quickest win in local search. It's free, directly controlled by you, and the ranking impact is immediate.

If you already have a claimed and verified GBP, jump to "Optimization" below. If not, follow the setup section first.

## Setting Up Your GBP From Scratch

### Step 1: Create or Claim Your Listing

1. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com)
2. Sign in with a Google account your firm controls. **Use a firm-owned Google Workspace account, not a personal Gmail.**
3. Click "Add your business" or search for your firm name
4. If your firm already appears in Google's directory, click "Claim this business"
5. If not, click "Create a new business" and enter your legal firm name exactly as it appears on your business license
6. Enter your complete physical address. You must have a physical office address. (Mailbox services don't count.)
7. Enter your primary phone number and website URL
8. Select your verification method:
   - **Postcard by mail** (5–14 days) — most common
   - **Phone verification** — sometimes available
   - **Video verification** — Google will ask you to record your office
   - **Email verification** — for verified domain owners

Complete verification immediately. Do not edit your business name or address while verification is pending.

### Step 2: Primary Category Selection

Your primary category tells Google what your firm is. This is your highest-leverage field. Choose wrong and you'll rank for the wrong searches.

**Select based on your highest-revenue practice area:**

| If your main practice is: | Choose this primary category |
|---|---|
| Personal Injury | Personal Injury Attorney |
| Criminal Defense | Criminal Justice Attorney |
| Family/Divorce | Divorce Lawyer |
| Estate Planning | Estate Planning Attorney |
| Immigration | Immigration Attorney |
| Real Estate | Real Estate Attorney |
| DUI/DWI | DUI Attorney |
| Bankruptcy | Bankruptcy Attorney |
| General Practice | Law Firm |

**The rule:** One primary category. Choose your practice area that generates the most revenue and leads. This is not the place to hedge.

### Step 3: Add Secondary Categories

You can add up to 9 secondary categories. Use all of them to capture your secondary practice areas.

If you handle: personal injury, workers' compensation, and medical malpractice — add:
- Primary: Personal Injury Attorney
- Secondary: Medical Malpractice Attorney, Workers' Compensation Attorney

## Optimization: Field-By-Field Guide

Once your GBP is claimed and verified, optimize every field. This is not a "nice to have" exercise — each field is a ranking and conversion signal.

### Business Name

Your legal business name only. No keywords, no location modifiers.

**✅ Correct:**
- Smith & Associates
- Johnson Law Firm
- Carter Litigation Group

**❌ Incorrect:**
- Smith & Associates Personal Injury Lawyers Tampa
- Best Criminal Defense Attorneys Near Me
- Johnson Law Firm | Call 24/7

### About Section (Business Description)

You have 750 characters. Use all of them.

**Formula:**
> [Firm name] is a [practice area] law firm serving [city/region]. We specialize in [specific case types] for [client type]. With [credential/differentiator], we [value proposition]. Contact us for a [type of consultation].

**Example (720 characters):**
> Smith & Associates is a personal injury law firm serving Tampa and the surrounding communities. We represent injury victims in car accidents, truck accidents, slip-and-fall cases, and wrongful death claims. Our attorneys have recovered over $50 million in verdicts and settlements for our clients. We work on contingency — you pay nothing unless we win. Available 24/7 for emergency consultations. Call (813) 555-0100 or visit our website for a free case review.

**Rules:**
- Use keywords naturally (personal injury, criminal defense, etc.) but don't stuff
- Include your city and service area
- Mention specific case types
- Include a differentiator (years of experience, awards, results)
- Include phone number
- No all-caps, no promotional language ("best," "#1," etc.)
- No URLs (not allowed in description)

### Services Section

Google suggests services based on your category. Accept relevant ones and add custom services for specific case types.

For a personal injury firm, add:
- Car Accident Claims
- Truck Accident Claims
- Motorcycle Accident Claims
- Slip and Fall Claims
- Medical Malpractice Claims
- Wrongful Death Claims

For each service, add a 1-2 sentence description:

> "We represent car accident victims throughout Tampa and Hillsborough County. No fees unless we win. Call for a free consultation."

### Hours

Accurate hours are critical. If your hours are wrong, potential clients will think you're closed or unreachable.

Enter your standard business hours. If you're available 24/7 for emergencies:
- List your office hours normally
- Add this to your business description: "Available 24/7 for emergency consultations"

### Phone Number

Use the main phone number potential clients should call to reach you. One number only.

**Best practice:** Use a dedicated line that goes to your intake team, not a general reception number that might transfer calls around.

### Website URL

Enter your main website URL. Make sure it's correct and the website is live.

### Photos

Photos are underutilized and high-impact. Listings with photos get significantly more direction requests and website clicks.

**Target: 25+ photos**

| Category | Count | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Exterior | 5 | Building front, entrance, signage, parking lot, street view |
| Interior | 8 | Reception area, conference rooms, offices, law library, hallways |
| Team | 8 | Attorneys at desks, attorneys in action, team photos, candid office moments |
| Office Details | 4 | Awards, certifications on walls, reception desk, conference table |

**Best practices:**
- Use high-quality photos (at least 1200 x 900 pixels)
- Vary angles and lighting
- Include people when possible (adds trust)
- Show your actual office, not stock photos
- Add captions to each photo (helps indexing)
- Upload 2–3 new photos per month to show activity

### Business Attributes

Google presents attributes (things like "Wheelchair accessible," "Video consultations available," etc.) based on your category. Set these accurately.

Tick "true" for:
- Video consultations (if you offer them)
- Wheelchair accessible (if you are)
- Accepts credit cards (if relevant)

Don't artificially inflate attributes. Google tracks mismatches and penalizes them.

### Q&A Section

Google's Q&A feature lets customers ask questions and you respond. This is an underused ranking opportunity.

**Monitor your Q&A weekly.** Answer every question within 24 hours. Use questions as an opportunity to reinforce keywords and mention practice areas.

**Example:**

Q: "Do you handle DUI cases?"
A: "Yes, we specialize in DUI and DWI defense in Tampa. Our attorneys have over 50 years of combined experience in criminal defense. Call (813) 555-0100 for a free consultation."

### Posts

Google Business Profile posts are short updates (up to 300 characters) that appear on your listing. They're also a slight ranking signal.

**Post frequency:** 1–2 posts per month minimum

**Post types:**
- Case results (anonymized): "Just recovered $50K for our client in a car accident case. Call us for a free evaluation."
- Practice tips: "Did you know? Florida's statute of limitations for personal injury is 4 years. Don't wait to call."
- Legal news: "New Florida law changes DUI penalties. Here's what you need to know..."
- Office updates: "We're expanding to a new office location. Same great service, better location!"

### Messaging (Optional but Recommended)

Google allows potential clients to send you messages directly through your GBP. Enable this if you have someone who can respond within 24 hours.

# Chapter 3: Platform Decision: WordPress vs. Static Sites (Astro)

This is the most important decision in this playbook. Your choice cascades through everything else — speed, maintenance cost, security, and your ability to scale.

## What We're Deciding

**WordPress:** A content management system (CMS) that powers ~43% of the web. It's flexible, has thousands of plugins, and lets non-technical people manage content.

**Static Sites (Astro):** A modern framework that generates ultra-fast, ultra-secure websites. You write content in markdown, build once, deploy everywhere.

Most law firms use WordPress. Most law firms are also leaving performance, security, and cost on the table.

## The Head-to-Head Comparison

### Performance (Speed)

**WordPress:**
- Average page load: 2–4 seconds
- Relies on plugins for caching and optimization
- Database queries on every page load
- Adds 200-500KB+ in code per page
- Degrades as you add plugins

**Static Sites (Astro):**
- Average page load: 0.5–1 second
- No database, no plugins needed
- HTML delivered directly to browser
- 30-80KB per page
- Speed doesn't degrade with content

**Why it matters:** Google ranks faster sites higher. A 1-second improvement in load time can improve rankings noticeably. For users: faster sites convert better. A law firm website that loads in 1 second vs. 3 seconds will convert more leads, period.

**Winner:** Static sites, decisively. WordPress can be fast with optimization, but it requires expertise and ongoing maintenance. Static sites are fast by default.

### Security

**WordPress:**
- Runs on a web server that executes code on every request
- Requires constant plugin and core updates
- Vulnerable to SQL injection, XSS attacks, and plugin vulnerabilities
- Requires security plugins and monitoring
- Requires backups and disaster recovery procedures

**Static Sites (Astro):**
- Generates static HTML files (no code execution)
- No database to hack
- No login system (unless you add one)
- No plugins to compromise
- Hosted on ultra-secure CDNs (Vercel, Netlify)

**Why it matters:** Your website contains potential client information. If you're collecting leads (names, emails, phone numbers), security matters. WordPress requires ongoing vigilance. Static sites are inherently secure.

**Winner:** Static sites. Security is baked in, not bolted on.

### Cost

**WordPress:**
- Hosting: $10–$50/month
- SSL certificate: $0–$100/year (often free)
- Security plugins: $100–$500/year
- Performance plugins: $100–$300/year
- Backups: $50–$200/year
- Theme/customization: $100–$1000+ (one-time)
- **Total: $500–$2000+/year**

**Static Sites (Astro):**
- Hosting (Vercel): $0–$20/month
- Domain: $10–$15/year
- SSL certificate: Free (automatic)
- Theme/design: Included
- No plugins, no add-ons
- **Total: $120–$240/year**

**Why it matters:** A static site costs 80% less to operate. That money can go toward content creation, advertising, or your bottom line.

**Winner:** Static sites, by a large margin.

### Maintenance & Reliability

**WordPress:**
- Requires weekly plugin updates
- Requires core updates every 1–2 weeks
- Downtime risk from plugin conflicts
- Requires regular backups
- Requires database maintenance
- Crashes when traffic spikes
- Requires technical expertise to manage

**Static Sites (Astro):**
- No updates needed (ever)
- No downtime (hosted on global CDNs)
- Handles traffic spikes automatically
- No backups needed (version control is your backup)
- No database maintenance
- Can be managed by non-technical staff (edit markdown, push to GitHub)

**Why it matters:** Your website should be reliable. If your website goes down because of a plugin conflict or traffic spike, you're losing leads. Static sites don't crash.

**Winner:** Static sites, decisively.

### Content Flexibility

**WordPress:**
- ✅ Easy to edit content via admin dashboard
- ✅ Non-technical users can manage pages
- ✅ WYSIWYG editor (what you see is what you get)
- ❌ Hard to version control content
- ❌ Hard to collaborate on changes
- ❌ Hard to roll back mistakes

**Static Sites (Astro):**
- ✅ Content stored in version control (GitHub)
- ✅ Easy to track changes and who made them
- ✅ Easy to roll back mistakes
- ✅ Easy for teams to collaborate
- ❌ Requires someone comfortable with markdown and GitHub
- ❌ Requires rebuilding site to publish (usually <1 minute automated)

**Why it matters:** If your firm is small and one person manages the site, WordPress is easier. If you want version control, collaboration, and safety — static sites win.

**Verdict:** Static sites win here for sophisticated teams. WordPress wins for simplicity if you don't care about version control.

### Customization & Extensibility

**WordPress:**
- ✅ Thousands of plugins and themes
- ✅ Easy to add features
- ❌ Plugin ecosystem is inconsistent (quality varies wildly)
- ❌ Plugins often conflict
- ❌ Heavy customization requires code

**Static Sites (Astro):**
- ✅ Modern, clean architecture
- ✅ Easy to add custom code
- ✅ No plugin bloat
- ❌ Smaller ecosystem
- ❌ Requires developer knowledge for custom work

**Verdict:** WordPress is more flexible. But that flexibility comes at a cost — complexity and maintenance burden.

## The Verdict: Static Sites (Astro) for Law Firms

**Recommendation: Use Astro or a similar static site framework for your law firm website.**

Here's why:

1. **Performance matters for ranking and conversion.** Static sites are inherently faster. Google rewards speed. Users convert better on fast sites.

2. **Cost is 80% lower.** That's money you're leaving on the table with WordPress.

3. **Security is non-negotiable.** A hacked legal website is a reputation disaster. Static sites eliminate entire categories of attacks.

4. **Reliability is critical.** Your website should never go down. Static sites don't crash.

5. **You don't need WordPress plugins.** Most law firms don't need complex functionality. They need fast, secure, professional websites.

6. **Modern frameworks are designed for this.** Astro, Next.js, Hugo, and similar tools are purpose-built for fast, secure websites. They're not "simple blog generators" anymore.

## When WordPress Makes Sense

If your firm:
- Needs non-technical staff to edit content daily without rebuilds
- Requires complex e-commerce or membership functionality
- Doesn't have anyone comfortable with GitHub or version control
- Has existing WordPress-specific plugins you can't replicate

Then WordPress might be the right choice. But you'll pay for that convenience in performance, security, and cost.

## Implementation Path

**If you're choosing Astro:**
1. Pick a hosting platform (Vercel or Netlify — both are free for small sites)
2. Use a template (many law firm templates exist for Astro)
3. Set up content editing in markdown or a headless CMS like Supabase
4. Deploy (automatically rebuilds your site when you update content)

**If you're staying with WordPress:**
1. Invest in performance optimization (caching plugins, CDN, image optimization)
2. Invest in security (security plugins, regular backups, monitoring)
3. Limit plugins (each plugin is a potential vulnerability and performance hit)
4. Plan to pay more for hosting and maintenance

- [ ] If you're staying with WordPress: Implement WP Super Cache or Cloudflare for performance

# Chapter 4: Practice Area & Location Pages — The Real Ranking Engine

Your homepage ranks for your firm name. Your practice area and location pages rank for the searches that actually matter.

Most law firm websites have a homepage and a blog. That's it. They're missing the pages that capture 70% of legal search traffic: practice area pages and location pages.

## Why These Pages Matter

When someone searches "personal injury lawyer Tampa," Google returns results for:
1. Practice area pages (about personal injury law)
2. Location pages (about Tampa)
3. Combinations of both (personal injury + Tampa)

If your website has no practice area pages, you can't rank for those searches. If you have no location pages, you can't rank for local searches.

This is the biggest missed opportunity in law firm marketing.

## Practice Area Page Structure

You need one page per practice area your firm actively handles. A criminal defense firm with 3 practice areas needs 3 practice area pages minimum.

### Page Title

Use the format: `[Practice Area] Attorney | [Your Firm Name] | [City]`

Examples:
- "Personal Injury Attorney | Smith Law | Tampa"
- "Criminal Defense Lawyer | Johnson & Associates | Florida"
- "Divorce Lawyer | Carter Law Group | St. Petersburg"

### Meta Description

150–160 characters, written for humans, not robots.

Example:
> "Personal injury attorney in Tampa specializing in car accidents, truck accidents, and slip-and-fall cases. Free consultation. Contingency fees."

### H1 Header

One H1 per page, matching your practice area.

Example: "Personal Injury Attorneys Serving Tampa & Hillsborough County"

### Content Structure

**Section 1: Practice area overview (200 words)**

What this practice area is. Who needs this service. Why it matters.

> "A personal injury case arises when you're injured due to someone else's negligence or wrongdoing. This includes car accidents, workplace injuries, slip-and-fall accidents, medical malpractice, and more. If you've been injured, you may be entitled to compensation for medical bills, lost wages, pain and suffering, and other damages."

**Section 2: Specific case types (300–400 words)**

Break down the types of cases you handle. Create a subsection for each type.

> #### Car Accident Cases
> [Explain what these are, what compensation looks like, what we do differently]
> 
> #### Motorcycle Accident Cases
> [Explain...]
> 
> #### Slip and Fall Cases
> [Explain...]

**Section 3: Your approach (200–300 words)**

How you differ from other firms. What you do for clients.

> "At Smith Law, we don't view injury cases as file numbers. We understand the financial and emotional toll of an injury. We handle everything — medical records, insurance negotiations, depositions, trials — so you can focus on recovery. We work on contingency. You pay nothing unless we win."

**Section 4: Why choose us (200 words)**

Credentials, experience, results, certifications.

> "Our personal injury attorneys have recovered over $50 million for injury victims. We're Board Certified in Civil Trial Law. We've tried over 200 cases to verdict. When insurance companies see our name, they negotiate seriously."

**Section 5: Call to action**

A clear next step.

> "If you've been injured, you have a limited time to take action. Call (813) 555-0100 for a free consultation. We'll evaluate your case and explain your options with no pressure."

### Target Length

800–1200 words. Long enough to be comprehensive. Short enough that people actually read it.

### Internal Linking

Link to related pages:
- Link to location pages (if you serve multiple cities)
- Link to related practice areas (personal injury → workers comp)
- Link to your blog (if you have relevant posts)

**Example:**
> "Motorcycle accidents often result in serious injuries. [Read about motorcycle accident liability here](/blog/motorcycle-accident-liability)."

## Location Page Structure

You need one page per geographic area you serve. If you serve Tampa, St. Petersburg, and Brandon — you need 3 location pages.

### Page Title

`[Practice Area] Attorney in [City] | [Your Firm Name]`

Examples:
- "Personal Injury Attorney in Tampa | Smith Law"
- "Criminal Defense Lawyer in St. Petersburg | Johnson & Associates"
- "Divorce Lawyer in Brandon | Carter Law Group"

### Meta Description

150–160 characters.

Example:
> "Personal injury attorney in Tampa handling car accidents, slip-and-fall cases, and wrongful death claims. Free case evaluation."

### H1 Header

`[Practice Area] Attorney in [City]`

### Content Structure

**Section 1: Local introduction (150 words)**

Why your firm is in this city. Local ties, local knowledge.

> "Smith Law has served the Tampa community for 20 years. We're located in downtown Tampa, just blocks from the Hillsborough County Courthouse. Our office is convenient to downtown, Ybor City, and South Tampa. We understand Tampa's roads, neighborhoods, and the local courts system."

**Section 2: Service area explanation (100 words)**

What geographic area you cover from this location.

> "From our Tampa office, we serve personal injury victims throughout Hillsborough County, including Tampa, Carrollwood, South Tampa, Temple Terrace, and Plant City. We also serve Pinellas County (St. Petersburg, Clearwater) and Pasco County (Wesley Chapel)."

**Section 3: Practice areas from this location (300 words)**

Link to practice area pages.

> "Our Tampa personal injury attorneys handle:
> - [Car Accident Cases](/personal-injury-car-accidents)
> - [Slip and Fall Cases](/personal-injury-slip-and-fall)
> - [Motorcycle Accident Cases](/personal-injury-motorcycle-accidents)"

**Section 4: Local results (200 words)**

Verdicts and settlements from this area if you have them.

> "Our Tampa office has recovered over $25 million for injury victims in Hillsborough and Pinellas counties. Recent results include a $2.3 million verdict in a truck accident case, a $1.8 million settlement in a medical malpractice case..."

**Section 5: Local team (optional, 150 words)**

Attorneys who work from this location, if you highlight individual attorneys.

**Section 6: Office information & contact**

Address, phone, hours, directions, parking info.

**Section 7: Call to action**

Clear next step.

### Target Length

600–1000 words.

Before you write a single page, inventory what you need.

**Formula:**
(Number of practice areas) × (Number of locations) = Minimum pages needed

**Example:**

| | Tampa | St. Pete | Brandon |
|---|---|---|---|
| Personal Injury | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Criminal Defense | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| Family Law | 1 | 1 | 1 |
| **Total pages:** | 3 | 3 | 3 |
| **Pages needed: 9** |

That's 9 dedicated pages (beyond homepage, about, blog, contact). If you're not writing these pages, you're not capturing these searches.

# Chapter 5: Website Architecture, Speed & Core Web Vitals

Your website architecture affects both user experience and rankings. Poor architecture leads to slow sites, poor navigation, and missed ranking opportunities.

## Site Structure Basics

Your site hierarchy should be:

```
Home
├── About
├── Practice Areas
│   ├── Personal Injury
│   ├── Criminal Defense
│   ├── Family Law
│   └── etc.
├── Locations
│   ├── Tampa
│   ├── St. Petersburg
│   └── Brandon
├── Blog
│   ├── Post 1
│   ├── Post 2
│   └── etc.
├── Contact
└── Resources
    ├── Forms
    ├── FAQs
    └── Playbook downloads
```

**Rules:**
- No more than 3 clicks from homepage to any page
- Practice area pages one level deep
- Location pages one level deep
- Blog posts one level deep
- Clear navigation (breadcrumbs help)

## Core Web Vitals

Google uses three metrics to measure user experience. These directly impact your ranking.

### Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)

How fast the main content loads. Target: **under 2.5 seconds**.

**How to improve:**
- Use a fast hosting provider (Vercel, Netlify)
- Optimize images (compress, use modern formats like WebP)
- Defer non-critical CSS and JavaScript
- Use a CDN (content delivery network)

**For WordPress:**
- Use WP Super Cache or W3 Total Cache
- Enable Cloudflare (free)
- Use an image optimization plugin (Smush)
- Defer YouTube embeds and third-party scripts

**For Astro:**
- Image optimization is automatic
- Hosting on Vercel/Netlify includes CDN
- Modern code delivery by default

### First Input Delay (FID)

How responsive your site is to user interaction. Target: **under 100ms**.

**How to improve:**
- Remove render-blocking JavaScript
- Minimize JavaScript execution
- Defer third-party scripts (analytics, chatbots, etc.)
- Use Code-Splitting (load JavaScript only when needed)

### Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)

How stable your layout is while loading. Target: **under 0.1**.

**How to improve:**
- Set image dimensions (don't let images resize)
- Avoid inserting ads and pop-ups above the fold
- Use CSS transforms instead of layout changes
- Preload fonts (don't let text shift while fonts load)

## Mobile Optimization

**Fact:** 70% of legal searches happen on mobile.

Your site must be fast and usable on phones.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Viewport meta tag is set (makes mobile layout work)
- [ ] Text is readable (minimum 16px font)
- [ ] Buttons and links are tap-able (minimum 48x48px)
- [ ] Forms are mobile-friendly (large inputs, visible labels)
- [ ] No horizontal scrolling
- [ ] Pop-ups don't block content on mobile
- [ ] Menu is accessible without scrolling
- [ ] Images are responsive (scale to device)

**Test:** Use Google's Mobile-Friendly Test (search.google.com/mobile-friendly) and verify every page passes.

## Image Optimization

Unoptimized images are the #1 cause of slow websites.

**Rules:**
1. **Compress images** (reduce file size without losing quality)
   - Use TinyPNG.com or Compressor.io
   - Aim for <200KB per image
2. **Use modern formats** (WebP instead of JPG/PNG)
   - WebP is 25–35% smaller
   - Browsers support it (IE is not used for legal research)
3. **Set image dimensions** (prevent layout shift)
   - Always specify width and height in HTML
4. **Use responsive images** (different sizes for mobile vs. desktop)
   - Use `srcset` attribute
   - Serve smaller images to mobile devices
5. **Lazy load below-the-fold images**
   - Don't load images until user scrolls to them

**Tools:**
- TinyPNG.com (compression)
- Squoosh.app (format conversion)
- ImageOptim (batch processing)

## Page Speed Testing

Test your site regularly:

**Tools:**
- [Google PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/) — the official Google tool
- [GTmetrix.com](https://gtmetrix.com/) — detailed breakdown
- [WebPageTest.org](https://www.webpagetest.org/) — advanced testing

**Targets:**
- LCP: <2.5s (Core Web Vital)
- FID: <100ms (Core Web Vital)
- CLS: <0.1 (Core Web Vital)
- Overall load time: <3 seconds
- Mobile speed: <4 seconds

If your site doesn't meet these, address it before launching. Speed directly impacts rankings and conversions.

# Chapter 6: Content Strategy — What To Write and When

Content serves two purposes: it ranks your site, and it convinces prospective clients you know what you're talking about.

Most law firm content is either corporate puffery ("We're passionate about justice") or non-existent. Neither strategy works.

## Content Tiers

Organize your content into three tiers:

### Tier 1: Cornerstone Content (Practice & Location Pages)

These are your practice area and location pages (Chapter 4). They're the foundation.

**Frequency:** Write once, update quarterly
**Length:** 800–1200 words
**Purpose:** Rank for main practice area keywords
**Examples:** "Personal Injury Attorney in Tampa," "Criminal Defense Lawyer"

### Tier 2: Topic Clusters (Blog Posts Around Practice Areas)

These are detailed blog posts about specific subtopics within your practice areas.

**Frequency:** 2–4 posts per month
**Length:** 1200–2000 words
**Purpose:** Rank for specific questions and case types within your practice area
**Examples:** 
- "How Much Is My Car Accident Case Worth?"
- "What Should I Know About DUI Checkpoints?"
- "How Long Does a Divorce Take in Florida?"

**Strategy:** Each cornerstone page should have 4–8 related blog posts linking back to it.

### Tier 3: Quick Answer Content (FAQ, How-To, News)

Short, specific answers to common questions. These rank for long-tail searches.

**Frequency:** 1–2 posts per week
**Length:** 400–800 words
**Purpose:** Answer specific questions, capture long-tail searches
**Examples:**
- "What Damages Can I Recover in a Personal Injury Case?"
- "How to Handle a Police Traffic Stop"
- "What to Do If You're Injured at Work"

## Content Calendar

Plan your content month by month. Don't write randomly.

**Sample monthly plan:**

| Week | Content Type | Topic | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Blog Post | "Top Reasons Car Accident Claims Get Denied" | 1500 words |
| Week 2 | FAQ | "How Long Does It Take to Settle a Car Accident?" | 600 words |
| Week 3 | Blog Post | "Medical Records and Your Personal Injury Case" | 1500 words |
| Week 4 | Quick Answer | "What to Do At the Scene of an Accident" | 500 words |

**Rules:**
- Distribute content across your practice areas
- Don't post 3 blogs one week and nothing for 2 weeks
- Consistency beats quantity (regular posting > sporadic marathon sessions)
- Each month, your blog should have 8–12 new pieces

## Keywords & Topic Research

Don't guess what people search for. Use tools.

**Free tools:**
- Google Autocomplete (start typing in Google, see what auto-suggests)
- Google Search Console (see what you currently rank for)
- AnswerThePublic.com (see questions people ask)
- Ubersuggest Free (limited but useful)

**Paid tools (worth the investment):**
- SEMrush ($99–$500/month)
- Ahrefs ($99–$400/month)
- Moz Pro ($99–$599/month)

**Process:**
1. Pick your main topic ("car accidents")
2. Find keyword variations ("car accident settlement," "car accident attorney," etc.)
3. Identify related questions ("how much is my case worth," etc.)
4. Write content to answer those questions
5. Link that content back to your main practice area page

## Internal Linking Strategy

Internal links tell Google how your content relates. They also distribute authority through your site.

**Rule:** Every blog post should link to 2–3 of your practice area or location pages.

**Example:**
- Blog post: "How Much Is My Car Accident Case Worth?"
  - Links to: `/practice-areas/personal-injury/`
  - Links to: `/practice-areas/personal-injury/car-accidents/`

**Additional links:** Link related blog posts together.

- Blog post: "Car Accident Settlement Process"
  - Links to: "How Much Is My Car Accident Case Worth?"
  - Links to: "What to Do at the Scene of an Accident"

## Content Quality Standards

**Every piece of content should meet these standards:**

1. **Accurate:** Cite sources, verify facts, consult with attorneys
2. **Complete:** Answer the question fully (not just the intro)
3. **Well-structured:** Use headers, bullets, short paragraphs
4. **Optimized:** Include keywords naturally, not forced
5. **Actionable:** Give readers something to do
6. **Mobile-friendly:** Readable on phones (which is 70% of visits)
7. **Free of jargon:** Explain legal terms in plain English
8. **Credible:** Include author bio, credentials, call to action

**Writing checklist:**
- [ ] Read it out loud — does it sound natural?
- [ ] Does it answer the headline question?
- [ ] Are there at least 3 internal links?
- [ ] Is the first paragraph a clear summary?
- [ ] Are there subheaders for each section?
- [ ] Is the call to action clear?

# Chapter 7: Technical SEO & Schema Markup

Technical SEO is the invisible plumbing that makes search engines understand your site. Most law firms skip it. You shouldn't.

## XML Sitemaps

A sitemap tells Google every page on your site.

**What you need:**
- One XML sitemap for pages (sitemap.xml)
- One XML sitemap for images (image-sitemap.xml)
- One XML sitemap for videos (if applicable)

**Create a sitemap:**
- If using Astro/static site: builds automatically
- If using WordPress: use Yoast SEO or Google XML Sitemaps plugin
- Submit to Google Search Console

**Format example:**
```xml
<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<urlset xmlns="http://www.sitemaps.org/schemas/sitemap/0.9">
  <url>
    <loc>https://yourfirm.com/</loc>
    <lastmod>2026-07-15</lastmod>
    <changefreq>monthly</changefreq>
    <priority>1.0</priority>
  </url>
  <url>
    <loc>https://yourfirm.com/personal-injury/</loc>
    <lastmod>2026-07-10</lastmod>
    <changefreq>monthly</changefreq>
    <priority>0.8</priority>
  </url>
</urlset>
```

## Robots.txt

Tells search engines which pages to crawl.

**Typical robots.txt for a law firm:**
```
User-agent: *
Allow: /
Disallow: /admin/
Disallow: /wp-admin/
Disallow: /search/
Sitemap: https://yourfirm.com/sitemap.xml
```

## Meta Robots Tags

Control how Google treats individual pages.

**Common uses:**
- `<meta name="robots" content="noindex">` — Don't index this page (useful for thank-you pages, privacy pages)
- `<meta name="robots" content="nofollow">` — Don't follow links on this page
- `<meta name="robots" content="max-snippet:-1">` — Allow snippets of any length

**For a law firm:** Most pages should be indexable and followable. Only add meta robots tags when you have a specific reason.

## Canonicalization

If the same page is accessible via multiple URLs, tell Google which is the "canonical" version.

**Example:** Both `yourfirm.com` and `www.yourfirm.com` load the same page. Set one as canonical:

```html
<link rel="canonical" href="https://www.yourfirm.com/">
```

**Rule:** Always use www or non-www consistently, and set canonicals to match.

## Schema Markup (Structured Data)

Schema tells Google what type of content is on your page. For law firms, use:

### LocalBusiness Schema

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Attorney",
  "name": "Smith Law Firm",
  "image": "https://yourfirm.com/logo.png",
  "description": "Personal injury law firm serving Tampa",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street",
    "addressLocality": "Tampa",
    "addressRegion": "FL",
    "postalCode": "33602"
  },
  "telephone": "+1-813-555-0100",
  "url": "https://yourfirm.com",
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.google.com/maps/place/...",
    "https://www.facebook.com/yourfirm"
  ],
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.8",
    "ratingCount": "150"
  }
}
```

**Put this on your homepage and every practice area page.**

### Article Schema

For blog posts:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Article",
  "headline": "How Much Is My Car Accident Case Worth?",
  "description": "An attorney explains what factors determine car accident settlement amounts",
  "image": "https://yourfirm.com/images/car-accident.jpg",
  "datePublished": "2026-07-10",
  "dateModified": "2026-07-15",
  "author": {
    "@type": "Person",
    "name": "John Smith",
    "url": "https://yourfirm.com/about/john-smith"
  }
}
```

**Put this on every blog post.**

### FAQPage Schema

For FAQ pages:

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "How much does a personal injury attorney cost?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "Most personal injury attorneys work on contingency..."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

## How to Add Schema Markup

**For WordPress:**
- Use Yoast SEO plugin (automatically generates schema)
- Rank Math plugin (also automatic)
- Manual: add schema in the header or footer

**For Astro/static sites:**
- Add schema as JSON-LD in `<script>` tags
- Use a schema generator and paste the code
- Add schema in page templates (applies to all pages automatically)

**Tools:**
- Schema.org (official documentation)
- Google Rich Results Test (test.google.com/rich-results)
- Yoast Schema Generator (for manual schema)

## Mobile-First Indexing

Google crawls and indexes your site from a mobile perspective. Your desktop site doesn't matter if your mobile site is broken.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Mobile layout is readable (no horizontal scroll)
- [ ] Text is 16px minimum (readable without zooming)
- [ ] Buttons and links are tap-able (48x48px minimum)
- [ ] Mobile site has all the content of desktop site
- [ ] Mobile images load correctly
- [ ] Forms work on mobile

**Test:** Use Google Mobile-Friendly Test and verify every page passes.

## Page Indexing in Google Search Console

Google Search Console is your direct line to Google about your website.

**Set up GSC:**
1. Go to [search.google.com/search-console](https://search.google.com/search-console)
2. Add your property (your domain)
3. Verify ownership (via DNS record, HTML tag, Google Analytics, etc.)
4. Submit your sitemap (sitemaps section)
5. Monitor coverage (which pages are indexed)

**Monitor monthly:**
- Check "Coverage" report — are all your pages indexed?
- Check "Performance" report — what do you rank for?
- Monitor errors (crawl errors, structured data issues)
- Check "Mobile Usability" report — mobile issues
