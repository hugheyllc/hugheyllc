AI search—Google's AI Overviews, ChatGPT search, Perplexity—is changing how prospective legal clients find attorneys. The first page of Google looks different. And law firm SEO strategy needs to adapt.

But here's the good news: the fundamentals haven't changed. Content authority matters more than ever. Direct attorney expertise matters more than ever.

## What's Changed

### AI Overviews
Google now synthesizes answers from multiple sources at the top of results. For informational queries ("what should I do after a car accident?"), the AI Overview may answer the question without users clicking any individual result.

For transactional queries ("Tampa personal injury attorney"), AI Overviews are less dominant—traditional organic results and local pack still drive most clicks.

### ChatGPT Search & Perplexity
These AI-powered search engines pull from the web and cite sources. If your firm is cited, you get visibility. If you're not, you don't.

## The SEO Response

The question is no longer "will my site rank?" but "will my content be cited by AI systems as authoritative?"

### 1. Author Expertise Matters
AI systems favor content showing genuine first-hand knowledge. Blog posts authored by named attorneys with credentials, discussing specific cases and local procedures.

### 2. Original Research Matters
Content that synthesizes information differently than competitors. Case studies, data analysis, proprietary research. Stuff that's worth citing.

### 3. Comprehensive Content Matters
Longer, more authoritative content that covers topics deeply. Skimmed content won't be cited—thorough, expert-level content will be.

### 4. Content Authority Matters
A page written by a practicing attorney is worth more than the same page written by a content agency. E-E-A-T signals have always mattered for legal content; they matter even more now.

## Long-Term SEO Strategy

For law firms, the playbook is:

1. **Claim local pack presence** (Google Business Profile, directories)
2. **Build content authority** (blog posts written by attorneys, case analysis, local market insights)
3. **Develop citations** (be mentioned by other authoritative legal sources)
4. **Create original content** (case studies, research, unique perspectives on local market)

AI search isn't the death of SEO. It's the elevation of the most important part of SEO: genuine expertise and authority.
