“Getting more leads” is not the same as getting more cases. Many law firms invest heavily in PPC and SEO without knowing whether those dollars translate into retained clients. This blog shows how to map the journey from a click on an ad or search result to a paying client and why proper attribution matters.

## Understand Your Funnel

- **Clicks and Impressions.** Start at the top: measure impressions, click‑through rate (CTR) and cost per click (CPC). In 2024–25, the average CTR for legal search ads was **76%** and the average CPC ranged from **$9–$69** depending on practice area. Use this to benchmark your performance.
- **Landing Page Experience.** After the click, your landing page must load quickly and convey trust. A **one‑second delay** can reduce conversions by **7%**. Ensure pages are mobile‑optimized and include clear calls to action.
- **Lead Conversion.** Track form submissions, call tracking and chat inquiries. The average conversion rate for legal campaigns is around **7%**, but bankruptcy and tax law often exceed **5%**. Use long‑tail keywords and compelling offers (e.g., free consultations) to improve conversion.
- **Qualified Consultations.** Measure how many leads convert to booked consultations. Set a target cost per qualified consultation (CPQC) based on your expected case value.
- **Retained Clients.** Finally, track consult‑to‑retained ratio. Identify where prospects drop off—are they unqualified, priced out, or lost due to slow follow‑up? AI intake systems can reduce response times and improve conversion by **40%**.

## Build a Cost Model

- **Calculate Cost per Lead (CPL).** CPL = total ad spend ÷ number of leads. Accept a higher CPL if the conversion and case values justify it. Personal injury leads can cost **$700–$1,500** each, but high case values often justify the spend.
- **Calculate CPQC and Client Acquisition Cost (CAC).** CPQC = total marketing spend ÷ number of qualified consultations. CAC = total marketing spend ÷ number of retained clients. Compare CAC to client lifetime value (CLV).
- **Assess Marketing Efficiency.** Marketing efficiency = revenue generated ÷ marketing spend. Use this to prioritise channels and adjust budgets.

## Align Marketing and Finance

Marketing and finance must work together to set budgets and measure success. Allocate budgets based on your growth stage: 10% of revenue during growth, 20% during expansion and more for aggressive scaling. Use multi‑touch attribution models to credit all touchpoints along the funnel.

## Optimize Every Step

- **Improve targeting.** Use negative keywords and long‑tail phrases. Segment campaigns by practice area. Allocate 75% of your search budget to SEO and 25% to PPC for balanced growth.
- **Enhance landing pages.** Match landing page messaging to ad copy. Include trust signals (reviews, testimonials, security badges).
- **Automate follow‑up.** Implement AI chatbots and call routing to contact leads within five minutes. Use CRM automation to send reminders and nurture leads.
- **Refine offers.** Test consultation incentives, case evaluations or downloadable guides. Use A/B testing to see which offers convert better.
