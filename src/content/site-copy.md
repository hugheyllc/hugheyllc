## About Page

### Firm Narrative

Hughey LLC is a law firm marketing operations consulting firm founded in 2004. We work exclusively with law firms to build marketing systems that generate retained clients—not just leads, not just brand awareness, but the specific metric that matters: retained clients.

Twenty years in the legal marketing space has taught us one thing: most law firm marketing fails not because of the agencies or the tactics, but because of the disconnect between marketing operations and client intake. A campaign can look perfect in the agency report. The cost-per-click metrics can be excellent. The ad creative can be award-winning. And yet the retained client count doesn't move.

We solve for that disconnect.

Our approach combines three core capabilities: deep data analysis to understand what's actually driving clients, strategic direction that aligns marketing investment with intake capacity, and operational framework that connects every marketing channel to the metrics that matter for retained clients.

We serve law firms nationally, with particular expertise in personal injury, family law, business law, and complex civil litigation. Our home is Tampa Bay, Florida, where we work with firms across the Gulf Coast.

## Insights Section

### Insight 1: Law Firm Marketing Operating System

The most successful law firms don't think about marketing as a collection of channels and campaigns. They think about it as a system—an integrated set of processes, technologies, and people that consistently converts prospects into retained clients.

We call this the Marketing Operating System.

This framework includes: your marketing channels (SEO, PPC, directories, referrals), your intake process (forms, phone screening, qualification), your CRM (lead nurturing, follow-up), and your client retention data (which clients stay, which don't, why).

Most law firms have these pieces. Few have them connected.

This insight walks through how to think about marketing as a system, what the core components are, and how to integrate them so marketing spend actually correlates with client retention.

### Insight 2: SEO for Law Firms in an AI Search World

AI search—Google's AI Overviews, ChatGPT search, Perplexity—is changing how prospective legal clients find attorneys. The first page of Google looks different. And law firm SEO strategy needs to adapt.

But here's the good news: the fundamentals haven't changed. Content authority matters more than ever. Direct attorney expertise matters more than ever. The question is no longer "will my site rank?" but "will my content be cited by AI systems as authoritative?"

This insight covers: what AI search means for law firm visibility, how to position your firm to be cited by AI, what content strategy works in an AI-driven search landscape, and how to think about the long-term future of legal search.

### Insight 3: Intake System Integration

Your intake system is the connection point between marketing and client retention. It's where marketing leads become actual clients. And yet most law firms have outdated intake processes—paper forms, manual data entry, siloed information.

This insight covers: how to design an intake system that captures data critical for both client qualification and marketing attribution, what technology to use (and what to avoid), how to train intake teams, and how to connect intake data back to marketing so you actually know which channels drive the clients you want to keep.

### Insight 4: Legal Marketing KPIs That Actually Matter

Law firms measure a lot of marketing metrics: cost-per-click, impressions, click-through rate, cost-per-lead. But most of these metrics don't tell you what actually matters—which marketing activities drive retained clients.

This insight walks through: what metrics to track at each stage of the marketing funnel, how to calculate cost-per-retained-client by channel, what metrics to share with vendors and why, and how to set realistic KPIs that align marketing spend with business outcomes.

### Insight 5: Choosing a Marketing Partner

You need a marketing partner. But what kind? An SEO agency? A full-service digital agency? A marketing consultant? A fractional CMO? And how do you evaluate whether they're actually helping you grow?

This insight covers: different types of marketing partners and what they're good for, what questions to ask when evaluating a partner, red flags that indicate a partner isn't the right fit, and how to measure whether your marketing partner is actually driving business outcomes.
