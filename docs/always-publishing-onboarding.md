## Overview

This document outlines everything needed to launch the Always Publishing system for a new client. It covers what the client provides, what Hughey LLC sets up, and what the ongoing rhythm looks like once the system is live.

Typical launch timeline: **7–10 business days** from signed agreement to first published post.

| Item | Details | Priority |

| Social media access | LinkedIn page admin + Twitter/X credentials (see Part 1.3) | If social publishing included |

### 1.2 Firm Information

The client completes the **Firm Profile Intake Form** (provided separately). Key items:

- **Firm name** and any DBA names used in marketing
- **Practice areas** — list every area the firm actively takes cases in, ranked by priority
- **Geographic focus** — primary city, county, and any secondary markets
- **Ideal client profile** — who is the right client, who is not
- **Tone and voice** — formal, conversational, authoritative, approachable
- **Competitors to be aware of** — firms in the same market (for SEO positioning)
- **Cases or outcomes to avoid mentioning** — anything sensitive or off-limits
- **Bar state(s)** — which state bar(s) govern the firm's advertising
- **Board certifications** — any attorneys with official specialty certifications
- **Firm history and differentiators** — years in practice, notable background, what makes them different

### 1.3 Social Media Credentials (if applicable)

**Twitter/X:**
- App credentials are set up once by Hughey LLC and do not require client involvement after setup
- Client provides account login for initial authorization only

**LinkedIn:**
- Client adds Hughey LLC as Page Admin on the firm's LinkedIn Company Page
- Go to: LinkedIn Page → Admin tools → Manage admins → Add joe@joehughey.com

### 1.4 Legal & Compliance

- Confirm which state bar advertising rules apply (handled by Hughey LLC but client confirms)
- Confirm any internal firm policies on what can and cannot be published publicly
- Confirm whether the firm has any active disciplinary matters that would affect advertising (client self-disclosure)

### 1.5 Existing Content (if any)

- Share any blog posts, articles, or guides the firm has already published
- Share any content the firm started but never finished
- Note any topics that have already been covered and should not be duplicated

## PART 2: WHAT HUGHEY LLC DOES

### 2.1 Technical Setup (Week 1)

- [ ] WordPress plugin or CMS integration installed and tested
- [ ] Google Analytics goals/events reviewed and confirmed tracking
- [ ] Search Console baseline report pulled (current rankings, impressions, clicks)
- [ ] Google Business Profile audit completed
- [ ] Existing site content audited for gaps and keyword opportunities
- [ ] Bar compliance ruleset configured for client's state(s)
- [ ] Social publishing credentials configured and tested (if included)

### 2.2 Content Foundation (Week 1–2)

- [ ] Firm Profile Intake Form reviewed and processed
- [ ] Primary keyword list built (50–75 target terms across practice areas and geography)
- [ ] Content calendar drafted — first 90 days mapped out (posted in shared doc)
- [ ] Voice calibration: first 2–3 posts written and sent to client for approval before any publishing begins
- [ ] Client approves voice and style — adjustments made if needed

### 2.3 Ongoing Publishing (Week 3+)

- 2 posts per week published to the firm's website
- Each post is compliance-scanned before publishing
- Posts distributed to social channels per agreed schedule
- Monthly performance report sent to client (first 10 of each month)

## PART 3: COMPLIANCE PROCESS

Every piece of content is scanned against ABA Model Rules 7.1–7.5 and the client's applicable state bar advertising rules before it publishes.

**What the scanner checks:**
- Outcome guarantees ("you will win," "guaranteed results")
- Unsubstantiated superlatives ("best attorney," "top-rated")
- Missing disclaimers on past results
- Specialization claims without certification
- Comparative statements about other attorneys

**If a compliance issue is flagged:**
- The post is held — it does not publish
- The issue is identified with the specific language and a suggested fix
- Hughey LLC resolves the issue before publishing
- Client is notified only if the issue requires their input (rare)

## PART 4: SHARED WORKING DOCUMENT

Once onboarding begins, a shared Google Doc is created for each client containing:

- Firm Profile summary
- Content calendar (rolling 90 days)
- Published post log (title, URL, publish date, performance at 30/60/90 days)
- Keyword tracking list
- Compliance notes

Client can view and comment at any time. Hughey LLC updates it weekly.

## PART 6: WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

**Month 1–3:** Foundation phase
- Site has consistent fresh content
- Google begins indexing new posts
- Search Console shows impression growth on new URLs

**Month 3–6:** Traction phase
- Target keywords begin appearing in top 20–50 positions
- Organic traffic shows measurable growth
- Google Business Profile sees increased activity if posts are distributed there

**Month 6–12:** Compounding phase
- Multiple posts ranking in top 10 for target terms
- Organic traffic contributing to inbound leads
- Content library building authority signals across practice areas

**Important:** Content marketing compounds over time. The first 90 days build the foundation. Results accelerate in months 4–12. This is a long-term asset, not a short-term campaign.
